7 Destinations In India To Visit In The Monsoon

There are many places like Rishikesh or Haridwar that you might have visited many times or may not have. However, here you will learn about some more wonderful places to visit in the monsoon. These places have the accurate temperature and weather for a trip in this season.

Stay tuned to learn more about destinations in India to visit in the monsoon season.

Nelliampathi 

Nelliampathi is a wonderful hill station in Palakkad state, Kerala, with beautiful attractions. You will love the tea and coffee plantations all around, making it the best view in the monsoon. It is the best place for nature and wildlife lovers, as the green scenery looking hills will take all your attention.

All you need to do is hit the best two viewpoints, which are Kesavam Para and Sithar Kundu. The activities you can perform here are hiking, shopping, and sightseeing to enjoy your travels more. This place is ideal for couples, families, and friends, so everyone can visit it.

Agumbe

Agumbe is another monsoon-friendly destination situated in Thirthahalli taluka of Shimoga district in Karnataka. It is also known by another name, Cherrapunji, in South India, with heavy rainfall throughout the year. On average, you can experience 2,600 mm of rainfall in this area.

The conservation forest here is a sanctuary and home for King cobras. With beautiful wildlife, Agumbe also has the best spices with aromas and flavours. It is one of the best destinations for trekkers, and you can also go camping there. The main attractions of this place are the Barkana Falls, Onake Abbi Falls, and Jogigundi Falls.

Malshej Ghats

If you are a nature lover, you cannot miss the rare collection of flora and fauna in the Malshej Ghats in Maharashtra. This place gives you the beautiful sight of being located in the western Ghats. The monsoon is the perfect season to encounter the ultimate beauty of the Malshej Ghats.

Visit here with your loved ones and experience the beauty of nature in the rain. You can do multiple activities like Bird watching, camping, trekking, and sightseeing.

Kutch

Kutch, in Gujarat, gives you the best sight of clouds over Dhinodhar Hill in the monsoon. The place is a never-ending desert plain where you get to experience a bizarre horizon. It seems like a dream when you look at the beauty of Kutch submerged in the water.

For people who want to experience culture and enjoy sightseeing and camping, Kutch is their go-to destination.

Ranikhet 

Ranikhet in Uttarakhand is a lovely spot to spend your monsoon. The great Himalayan ranges can be seen with the greenery all around the hills and the pleasant climate. Again, it is one of the best places for nature and monsoon lovers.

Couples can also make their bond stronger with the majestic views from Ranikhet. You can go trekking and temple-hopping to make your trip more interesting. The place attracts you more with a Valley of flowers and endangered animals, including the Asiatic black bear, snow leopard, and birds.

Orchha 

Orchha is situated in Madhya Pradesh and was built by Raja Rudra Pratap in 1501. It is the ring of hills on the River Betwa where you can smell the sweet custard apples. It has towering temples and forts, making it the perfect spot to visit in the monsoon season.

If you are into history, this is the place you must visit and dig out more about its ancestry. This place attracts you with spots like Jahangir Palace, Riverside Cenotaphs, Rai Praveen Mahal, and Phool Bagh.

Goa 

Goa is one of the popular travel destinations that people find crazy to go to. In the monsoon, it becomes a scenic place where people get to enjoy beaches and a cool breeze. If you are a foodie and want to try some amazing food, Goa has delicious and authentic food.

You can make your monsoon trip wonderful with activities like Jet skiing, scuba diving, trekking, heritage tours, shopping, and birdwatching. You can enjoy the main attractions in Goa like trekking or hiking at Dudhsagar waterfalls, Aguada Fort for a sparkling view, a dolphin show, Cruises with beautiful sea surrounded by beaches, a casino, and water sports in Baga Beach.
