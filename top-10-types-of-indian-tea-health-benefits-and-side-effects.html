A Comprehensive Guide to India's Tea

Let us go through each Indian tea to help understand its benefits and side effects.

Passionflower Tea

Passionflower tea is a traditional recipe used by people to treat different problems in the human body. It is best to relieve anxiety and also improve sleep quality, as in research it was found that people who had passionflower tea for two weeks experienced some improvement in their insomnia.

If you are taking any dental treatment, having this tea will help relieve the symptoms of anxiety. You can have Passionflower tea to feel relaxation, enhance mood, sleep better, relieve pain, seizures, and hysteria.

Side Effects: As it has calming effects, having it at night is the best time, as during the day you might feel dizziness, drowsiness, mental slowing, and confusion. Some people might also feel vomiting and nausea after having this tea.

Rose Hip Tea

Rose hip is a red-coloured seed from which you can prepare tea. This plant and its seeds are beneficial for your health in many ways. The tea has a bold red colour and is rich in vitamin C. With its anti-inflammatory properties, your stomach doesn’t irritate you with inflammation.

In many studies, it was found that rose hip tea helps improve symptoms related to rheumatoid arthritis and osteoarthritis, which include pain. You can also drink this tea to manage your weight and BMI (body mass index).

Side Effects: A high dosage of rose hip tea can be harmful to your body. You might feel nausea, stomach cramps or pain, and diarrhoea. Also, high levels of vitamin C in rose hips can lead to kidney stones as your body converts vitamin C to oxalate.

Lemon Balm Tea

Lemon balm tea is made using lemon balm leaves, which have a lemony flavour. This soothing tea is great for health, consisting of several properties to promote health. Arterial stiffness is one of the factors for heart disease, stroke, or mental decline, which can be improved by consuming lemon balm tea for 6 weeks.

It has been found in studies that drinking lemon balm tea may help your body build its natural antioxidant enzymes and protect the body from oxidative stress. Lemon balm tea helps you handle depression and anxiety, and it won’t have any side effects in this case.


Side Effects: Lemon balm tea is beneficial; however, you might not feel right drinking it depending on your body mechanism. Side effects of lemon balm can include nausea, dizziness, sedation, increased appetite, wheezing, hypersensitivity reactions, and skin irritation.

Sage Tea

Sage is a plant that is used as a medicine to cure various health problems. Its leaves contain amazing properties that provide you with good health. It has potential advantages for brain health, and research has also shown that it improves the cognitive function of Alzheimer’s disease.

With proper intake of sage tea, studies have shown that it has improved adults memory and mental functioning. In studies, it was found that it lowers the total and LDL cholesterol levels, which takes care of your heart.

Side Effects: After drinking the sage tea, you might experience slow breathing and dizziness. This is true in some cases and also depends on the dosage. If you feel any of this, stop using Sage products and only start after consulting with your doctor.

Rooibos Tea

Rooibos is an ancient herb that was used to prepare different types of medicines. With its anti-allergic properties, you can cure many things going on in your body. However, more research is to be done to know its effects on humans and what it can cure.

Upon researching, Rooibos may also prevent the formation of osteoclasts, which are responsible for breaking the old bones. These cells are important in preserving, repairing, and reshaping the skeleton. Rooibos tea controls blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Side Effects: For some people, depending on the body mechanism, it can be harmful for the liver and kidney. You must get your full body checked before having this tea, as it might not be meant for you.

Echinacea Tea

Echinacea is a flower that is used to prepare Echinacea tea, providing various benefits. It is one of the best remedies to treat and prevent common colds and coughs. If you want to boost your immunity naturally, you must try Echinacea tea, which will prevent your body from harbouring several infections.

Echinacea contains different chemicals that include polysaccharides, glycoproteins, alkamides, volatile oils, and flavonoids. They provide healthcare in areas that need immediate attention, such as strengthening the immune system and preventing colds.

Side Effects: By having Echinacea tea, the most common side effects people experience are stomach pain, constipation, diarrhoea, heartburn, vomiting, and rash. Also, avoid applying it to the skin for long periods of time, as it can reverse the effects.

Hibiscus Tea

Hibiscus tea is a red-coloured tea with a soothing flavour and amazing health benefits. Its unique colour makes it more attractive, and it has medicinal properties that help you cure many problems going inside your body. Hibiscus tea helps you reduce cholesterol and control your blood pressure.

Studies have shown that regular intake of Hibiscus tea for 6 weeks significantly reduced oxidative stress in male soccer players. However, if you are on medication, especially taking diuretics, you must first consult your doctor before starting to drink hibiscus tea.

Side effects: Pregnant women must avoid this tea as it can be harmful for many reasons. There are other uncommon side effects, such as an upset stomach, a gastric problem, or even constipation. Before making it a habit, you must realise if there are any issues you face after drinking this tea.

Ginger Tea

Ginger tea is another common tea prepared in Indian homes. People also call this tea kadha when other herbs are added to it. With its antioxidant properties, your immune system gets stronger and is able to fight various diseases. It is one of the best remedies for nausea that women usually experience in PMS and pain during the menstrual cycle.

Also, it eases the feeling of nausea in early pregnancy and the nausea caused during cancer treatments or surgery. With its anti-inflammatory properties, your stomach is able to function properly, which also prevents stomach ulcers.

Side effects: It can cause dryness in your system if taken in excess quantities. Similarly, excess use of ginger can cause cardiac arrhythmias and abdominal discomfort. If you feel any of these, you must reduce the use of ginger and consult your doctor.

Peppermint Tea

Peppermint tea is another amazing tea you can add to your daily routine. If you face any digestive issues, you must try this tea, which solves various problems inside your body. It is rich in properties like antioxidants, anticancer, antibacterial, and antiviral.

Studies have shown that peppermint oil is best used to relieve indigestion, nausea, stomach pain, and irritable bowel syndrome. The natural form of peppermint is better to prepare the tea as compared to the supplements available on the market.


Side effects: Some people allergic to the peppermint might feel an itchy tongue and throat. Also, you might start sneezing and coughing if this substance doesn’t suit you. If you feel any of these symptoms, you must consult your doctor and stop the intake of this tea.

Chamomile Tea

Chamomile tea is a common tea that you will find in Indian homes. It is well-known for its calming and relaxing effects, and people drink it to sleep properly. Many case studies have shown that having this tea improves the quality of sleep in older people and women after giving birth to a child.

With its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, it can help you cure your liver. It has proved to be the cure for PMS (premenstrual syndrome). Chamomile tea also helps control your blood sugar levels.

Side Effects: The side effects vary from person to person with this chamomile tea. These effects are not common, but some people might feel nausea, an allergic reaction, or dizziness. To avoid dizziness, you must drink it at night before sleep and not during the day.
